# Business Insights & Recommendations

## Key Findings

### 1. Contract Type Impact
- **Month-to-Month contracts** show the highest churn rate (over 40%).
- Customers on One-year or Two-year contracts are significantly more loyal.
- **Insight**: Long-term commitment is the strongest predictor of retention.

### 2. Service Quality & Support
- Customers with **Fiber Optic** internet service have a higher churn rate compared to DSL users, possibly due to technical issues or competitive pricing.
- Customers who **do not have Tech Support** or Online Security are much more likely to churn.
- **Insight**: Value-added services are critical for "stickiness".

### 3. Financial Drivers
- Higher **Monthly Charges** correlate with increased churn risk.
- Customers in the "High" monthly revenue segment are more sensitive to price changes or service disruptions.
- **Insight**: Premium customers require proactive relationship management.

### 4. Tenure Trends
- Churn is highest in the **first 12 months** of the customer lifecycle.
- If a customer stays past 2 years, the probability of churn drops significantly.

## Strategic Recommendations

### 1. Incentive Long-term Contracts
- Offer discounts or bonus features for customers switching from month-to-month to annual contracts.

### 2. Target "At-Risk" Segments
- Use the **Risk Level** predictions to launch targeted retention campaigns.
- High Risk customers (Probability > 70%) should receive immediate outreach or special offers.

### 3. Promote Support Services
- Bundle Tech Support and Online Security into standard packages to improve customer satisfaction and reduce technical frustration.

### 4. Improve Fiber Optic Experience
- Investigate the specific reasons for high churn in the fiber optic segment (e.g., connection speed, reliability, or competitor pricing in those areas).
